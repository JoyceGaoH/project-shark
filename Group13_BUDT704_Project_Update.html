Project Overview¶

Shark Tank is a reality TV show which showcases startups pitching their ideas to wealthy investors (the Sharks'') aiming to convince them to invest in their startup. Over the years, the show has gained immense popularity amongst its viewers and keeps growing its user base year on year. Why? Because there is so much DRAMA. Most of these curated start-ups have something called the cocktail party factor, where everyone has an opinion about these businesses, which makes the show all the more relatable. Each year, the show is aired for a Season showcasing various start-up pitching for investments. Although the pitch is recorded long before it is aired, the airing of the pitch acts as a sweepstakes marketing campaign for the startups. Whilst pitching it to potential investors, the airing of the show makes it a pitch to their potential customers. The Shark Tank franchise is huge and spans across various countries. Although we love watching all of those shows, were going to focus on the iconic US Shark Tank.
The Sharks mentioned in the dataset are Barbara Corcoran, Mark Cuban, Lori Greiner, Robert Herjavec, Daymond John and Kevin O Leary.

Our objective is to proficiently process, analyze, and visualize data from diverse datasets, enabling us to extract valuable insights regarding patterns, behavior, pitch outcomes, temporal trends, and market intelligence.
We also aim to cover how this analysis will be useful, how the knowledge gained can be applied in real life and why this analysis was needed to be done.

As the team name DATA SHARKS suggests, we aim to fish the story out of these datasets and be able to finally understand the ultimate learning from this course - 'TELL ME A STORY!'.
At the end of it, we're gonna be like - "You're gonna need a bigger Jupyter Notebook!"

Research Questions ¶

Question 1:
Can we identify temporal patterns in pitch success on the show and how do they evolve over the seasons, including viewership trends?

Question 2:
Are there statistically significant co-investment patterns among the sharks, revealing insights into their investment strategies and price discrimination?

Question 3:
Can we analyze trends in pitchers on the show to identify sectors with the highest capital raised and assess how investment trends impact the business pitches?

Question 4:
What factors in Business pitches influence the equity demands of sharks on the show and to what extent do these descriptions impact the likelihood of securing a deal?

Question 5:
Does the presence of specific investors or guests on the show influence entrepreneurs' deal success and viewership, and who has the most significant impact on both?
